ETHIOPIA

The country that has never been colonized

Ethiopian History and the Victory of Adwa

    1896 - The Victory of Adwa

  • Ethiopian forces, led by Emperor Menelik II and Empress Taytu Betul, decisively defeat the Italian army at the Battle of Adwa. This victory ensures Ethiopia's sovereignty and makes it one of the only African nations to successfully resist European colonization.

    • 1844 - Menelik II is Born

  • Menelik II, future Emperor of Ethiopia, is born in Angolalla, Shewa.

    • 1889 - Menelik II Becomes Emperor

  • Following the death of Emperor Yohannes IV, Menelik II ascends to the throne and begins to consolidate his power and modernize the nation.

    • 1889 - Treaty of Wuchale

  • Menelik II signs the Treaty of Wuchale with Italy. A misunderstanding in the translation of the treaty’s language leads to the conflict that would culminate in the Battle of Adwa.

    • 1895 - Italian Invasion

  • Italy begins its invasion of Ethiopia, aiming to expand its colonial territories in the Horn of Africa. Menelik II calls upon the diverse ethnic groups of Ethiopia to unite against the common enemy.

    • 1896 - The Battle of Adwa

  • On March 1, 1896, the Ethiopian army achieves a landmark victory against the Italians at Adwa. This battle not only preserves Ethiopia’s independence but also serves as a symbol of resistance and hope for other colonized nations.

    • 1900 - Menelik II’s Modernization Efforts

  • Menelik II continues to modernize Ethiopia, introducing new technologies, infrastructure projects, and educational reforms to strengthen the nation.

    • 1913 - Menelik II Passes Away

  • Emperor Menelik II dies, leaving behind a legacy of unification, modernization, and resistance against colonialism.

    • 1930 - Haile Selassie I Becomes Emperor

  • Ras Tafari Makonnen is crowned Emperor Haile Selassie I. He continues the work of modernizing Ethiopia and becomes a prominent figure on the world stage.

    • 1935 - Italian Invasion and Occupation

  • Italy, under Mussolini, invades Ethiopia once again. Despite fierce resistance, Addis Ababa falls, and Ethiopia is occupied by Italy until 1941.

    • 1941 - Liberation of Ethiopia

  • Ethiopian patriots, with the support of British forces, successfully liberate Ethiopia from Italian occupation. Haile Selassie I returns from exile to lead the country once more.

    • 1963 - Formation of the OAU

  • The Organization of African Unity (OAU) is founded in Addis Ababa, with Haile Selassie playing a key role. The OAU works to promote unity and cooperation among African nations.

    • 1974 - End of Haile Selassie’s Reign

  • A military coup overthrows Emperor Haile Selassie, leading to the establishment of the Derg regime and a period of significant political and social upheaval in Ethiopia.

    • 1991 - Fall of the Derg

  • The Derg regime is overthrown by rebel forces, leading to the establishment of the Federal Democratic Republic of Ethiopia.

    • 1995 - New Constitution

  • Ethiopia adopts a new constitution, establishing a federal system of government and promoting ethnic autonomy and representation.

    • 2024 - Continuing Legacy

  • The legacy of the Victory of Adwa continues to inspire Ethiopians and people around the world, symbolizing the enduring spirit of resistance, unity, and independence.
